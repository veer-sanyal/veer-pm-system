# Scheduling Blueprint

## First principles

1. **Long-horizon consistency beats heroic surges.** A volume sustainable for 40 weeks beats one sustainable for 6.
2. **Cued behavior beats motivated behavior.** Implementation intentions (if-then cue -> action) are the best-evidenced bridge from intention to behavior. Commitments need a trigger, not a time.
3. **Visible progress is part of the work.** Monitoring strengthens when recorded and public. Tracking is the intervention.
4. **Recovery is execution, not reward.** Sleep, exercise, and breaks have measurable cognitive effects; skipping is a performance loss.
5. **Action interrupts rumination.** When Veer drifts into explanation or self-diagnosis, put a small physical action in front of him -- do not validate the analysis.

---

## Daily structure

**Deep work (75-90 min):** Hardest pillar work only (project / technical learning / interview prep -- not admin, email, planning). Same time and location daily. Phone out of reach; browser closed; project doc open from the night before. Cue format: "When I sit at [location] after [trigger], I open [project] and do [first move] for 25 minutes before deciding anything else." First 25 min non-negotiable.

**Secondary work (45-60 min):** Second-priority pillar; different category to reduce attention residue. Same cue + start-action structure.

**Small-but-visible item (15-30 min):** One shipped artifact -- alumni message, resume bullet, Anki review, practice problem out loud. Prevents zero-output days.

**Recovery -- not optional:** 7-8 hr sleep (deprivation impairs cognition; supports memory consolidation). Preserve workout and skincare at night (end-of-day cue). One genuine social or non-instrumental moment -- guards against "optimized but hollow" failure mode.

---

## Weekly structure

### Sunday planning (30-45 min) -- most important meeting of the week

1. Review: shipped / didn't / slipped (5 min)
2. Name the pattern -- not deep diagnosis (5 min)
3. Commitments by pillar: shipped, learned, sent, practiced (15 min)
4. Each -> implementation intention with a cue (10 min)
5. Block calendar: deep, secondary, alumni-outreach slots; draft daily events (10 min)

### Weekly volume targets (baselines; bias toward lower-and-consistent)

| Pillar | Target |
|---|---|
| Project work | 6-8 hr |
| Technical learning | 3-4 hr |
| Alumni outreach | 3 messages + 1 informational interview where possible (3/week for 20+ over the year) |
| Interview prep | 1 mock or 3 solo reps (out loud, recorded) |
| Sunday planning | 1 session |
| Friday review | 10 min -- right / slipped; no fixing yet |

### Recovery week every 6-8 weeks

Volume drops 40% -- required for 10-month survival. Sustained-stress cognition is impaired; intermittent recovery improves total output.

## Daily plan delivery (Google Calendar)

Gmail connector can only draft, not send or schedule -- morning email was never buildable. Calendar pushes to phone; full spec in daily-email-instructions.md.

Each day:
1. **Three work-block titles** naming the action so the reminder is the trigger (e.g., "Deep block: add telemetry to the dashboard (Pillar 1)").
2. **9:00 Morning Briefing** -- accountability question for yesterday ("Did the deep block start on time? If not, what got in the way?"), one-sentence "this week feeds," optional citation. Answer to Sunday or PROGRESS.md.
3. **One sentence of context** (not motivation): pillar served, why this week matters.

**Does NOT contain:** inspirational language; more than 3-4 items; anything not committed on Sunday; long-term goal reminders (over-reminding causes goal-gradient demotivation).

Recurring blocks: deep 9:45, secondary 11:45, workout 1:00, small item 2:30 (Pacific). Each Sunday retitles and writes the six briefings.

## Science-backed protocols

**Implementation intentions (Gollwitzer):** "When X, I do Y."

**WOOP / Mental contrasting + implementation intentions:** Sunday when a commitment is at risk -- wish, outcome, inner obstacle, if-then plan. Small-to-medium effect on goal attainment in meta-analysis.

**Proximal goals with feedback (Bandura, Schunk):** 10-month goal too distant. Weekly proofs: shipping, volume, skill progression, feedback gathered.

**Behavioral activation:** When Veer ruminates, drafts unsent messages, or refines instead of executing: one small physical action ("open project tracker, write one sentence"). Procrastination is short-term mood regulation -- task feels aversive, brain picks immediate relief, not laziness. Shrink the action to bypass aversion. Do not tell Veer to "be more disciplined."

**Pre-performance routines:** One minute slow exhale, cue phrase ("arousal is fuel"), glance at three anchor stories, deliberately slow opener on the first question.

**Reappraisal over suppression:** Framing anxiety as performance fuel improves outcomes; suppression degrades them.

**Expressive writing before high-stakes events:** 10 min writing about anxiety before mock interviews offloads worry and improves working memory.

**Overlearning + flexible retrieval:** Overlearn openings, scaffolds, core stories; practice flexible retrieval across variant prompts. Memorization alone is brittle; understanding alone is too slow.

**Friction design:** Phone out of reach during deep blocks; project doc open from night before; cue-environment paired (same location, same lighting).

## What the system does NOT do

- Track mood, energy, or feeling; track hours beyond pillar minutes
- Send motivational messages or adjust based on how Veer feels
- Add features for Veer's optimization urges
- Use streaks (cause perfectionism collapse; research supports recoverable consistency over unbroken chains)
- Punish missed days -- a missed day is data for Sunday

---

## When to adjust

**Sunday-level adjustment (not daily) when:**
- Same pillar slipping three weeks running
- Hitting volume but work isn't compounding (e.g., 8 hours on project, no shipped iteration)
- Burnout signals: sleep dropping, workout slipping, social withdrawal
- Plan executed mechanically without learning

**System is working when:**
- One protected deep block happens 5-6 days a week
- Veer can name what he shipped in the past week without checking
- Friday review shows visible compounding artifacts
- Alumni conversations are getting easier, not harder
- He is pushing back on the plan from execution, not avoidance
