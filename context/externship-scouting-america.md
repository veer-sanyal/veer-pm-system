## Project scope (from the one-pager)

**Title: Communication and Marketing Plan.**

The problem: the council has no communication/marketing plan. Channels exist (website, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, X, LinkedIn, email via Constant Contact, some print) but content is picked ad hoc ("no rhyme or reason"), driven by whatever event is next. No full-time marketing staff — communications are done by other staff with mixed results. Storytelling marketing ("the good work Scouting is doing") basically doesn't exist and should.

Desired content mix (their words, four buckets):
1. Event promotions
2. Celebrating achievements
3. Storytelling on program outcomes
4. Educating the community on the benefits of Scouting

Five distinct audiences: general public (awareness), business community (support), Scout families (internal), volunteer leaders (internal), donors (recognition + support).

**Deliverables asked for:**
1. A weekly/monthly/annual communications + marketing plan: posting schedule, content, frequency, channels/methods.
2. A list of adjustments to the council website + social accounts to better attract those audiences.

Resources offered: access to social accounts, Constant Contact, audience data, and their backlog list of events/topics. Competitors: none direct (Girl Scouts organizationally different; any youth-serving org loosely counts). Client explicitly wants a sit-down to discuss scope.
