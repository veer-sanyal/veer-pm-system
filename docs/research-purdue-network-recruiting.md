## (a) Which companies recruit Purdue for PM / PM-adjacent roles, and when

### The honest framing first
PM is a hard role to recruit *on-campus* anywhere, because most companies want demonstrated product sense, not a major. Purdue's strength is engineering + business volume hiring, so the realistic **on-campus** surface is PM-*adjacent*, and the **pure-PM** surface is **off-campus self-application** to APM programs. Treat these as two different funnels.

### Purdue's two main fairs (the on-campus surface)

- **Industrial Roundtable (IR)** — student-run by the Purdue Engineering Student Council; running since 1980; "one of the best career fairs in the nation," ~**400 companies** and **~19,000+** engineering/science/tech students; recent format is two in-person days plus a virtual day. **[SOURCED–PURDUE]** (https://www.purdueesc.org/industrial-roundtable ; https://www.careerfairplus.com/blog/how-to-engineer-annual-recruiting-wins-for-400-employers-and-12000-students-during-a-two-day-event). Named recent exhibitors include Texas Instruments, Cummins, Tate & Lyle, Sazerac, Southlake Automation, and Merck. **[SOURCED–PURDUE]** (https://www.purdueexponent.org/campus/industrial-roundtable-job-fairs-opportunities-tips-students-purdue-engineering-advice-recruiting/article_e3d3dd06-706c-11ef-b807-6b0ee557fcf6.html ; https://jobs.merck.com/us/en/event/689e2115a19afe7b4f45c52c/Purdue-Industrial-Roundtable-Career-Fair)

## (d) A research-backed plan: 20+ informational interviews in ~12 months

### Why the alumni angle is the whole game
**[SOURCED–GENERAL]**
- **Alumni cold outreach: ~20–40% response** vs **~5–15% for generic cold outreach.** (https://www.offerloop.ai/blog/alumni-networking-guide ; https://growleads.io/blog/warm-outreach-vs-cold-email/ ; https://sirjohnnymai.com/blog/alumni-database-vs-cold-linkedin-outreach/)
- **Plain LinkedIn outreach averages ~10%** reply; a **personalized connection note roughly doubles** acceptance vs none (~9.4% vs ~5.4%). (https://expandi.io/blog/state-of-li-outreach-h1-2026/ ; https://belkins.io/blog/linkedin-outreach-study)
- **Warm intro from a mutual contact: 70–90%** reply — so convert each call into a referral. (https://growleads.io/blog/warm-outreach-vs-cold-email/)
- **Conversion haircut:** even with good replies, expect **~60–70% of positive replies → an actual call.** (https://www.offerloop.ai/blog/alumni-networking-guide)

### The math (your target funnel) **[INFERENCE from the benchmarks above]**
Use a **conservative 25% alumni reply rate** and **65% reply→call**:
- 20 calls ÷ 0.65 = **~31 positive replies needed**
- 31 ÷ 0.25 = **~124 well-targeted alumni messages over 12 months**
- = **~10–11 personalized messages/month**, or **~3/week**. Very doable alongside coursework.
- Build in slack: aim to *send ~150* to comfortably clear 20+ calls, and front-load before the Aug–Nov 2026 application crunch.

### Message blueprint (Stanford-validated 5-part structure)
**[SOURCED–GENERAL]** (https://alumni.stanford.edu/career-connections/cold-contacting-an-alum-here-s-what-you-should-do/) — keep it to **4–6 sentences**:
1. **Who** you are (Purdue IBE '28, prior SWE internship).
2. **How** you found them (Purdue Alumni Tool / shared major / club).
3. **Why** them (specific: their PM path at [Company], a product they own).
4. **What's next:** ask for **20 minutes** in the next couple weeks, phone or Zoom.
5. **Thanks.**
**Avoid:** asking for a job/referral in the *first* message, sending a bare connection request with no note, anything generic. **[SOURCED–GENERAL]**

### Outreach cadence (per contact)
**[SOURCED–GENERAL]** (Stanford + outreach benchmarks)
- **Day 0:** personalized message (LinkedIn note ≤300 chars, or email if available).
- **Day +7:** if silent, one short follow-up — drop the ask to **15 minutes** or ask for a referral to someone more relevant.
- **Day +14:** if still silent, stop and move on. No third chase.
- **After each call:** thank-you within 24h; ask "**who else should I talk to?**" to manufacture warm intros (70–90% reply tier).

### 12-month operating rhythm **[INFERENCE]**
- **Months 1–2 (summer 2025-equivalent):** build the 150-name target sheet via the Alumni Tool + Boolean; join PM Club + Data Mine; ship one teardown.
- **Months 3–8 (your sophomore spring → summer 2026):** steady ~3 messages/week; bank referrals; attend Consulting/Data Mine fairs and PM Club speaker events (each speaker = a warm contact).
- **Months 9–12 (junior fall, Aug–Nov 2026):** convert relationships into **referrals** right as PM/APM windows open; ask your strongest 3–5 contacts directly to refer.
- **Tag every contact** by likely-can-refer vs informational-only, so you know whom to activate in the application window.
