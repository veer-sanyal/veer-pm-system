# LinkedIn Presence for a Student Aspiring PM (posting, content, personal brand)

The operating layer for **building a LinkedIn audience through posting and content** — the companion to `Genuine_Networking_for_Competitive_PM_Internships.md` (which owns DM outreach, reply-rate math, and the alumni message blueprint). This is "what to post and how the feed works." Run through a 3-vote adversarial-verification harness; the headline finding is about **reliability**, so read it first.

## The blunt headline: trust the mechanics, not the numbers

Of 25 high-value LinkedIn claims tested, **17 were refuted.** Almost every precise benchmark online is marketing-blog folklore that contradicts itself. **Refuted (do not plan around these):** all "best time/day to post" tables (Buffer, Hootsuite, Sprout each gave *conflicting* numbers); carousel/video engagement multipliers ("596% more", "5x video"); ideal character counts; the "60% external-link penalty" and the first-comment workaround; dwell-time-over-likes weighting; comment/share/reply weight rankings and the "15-minute reply boost"; "21x more profile views"; "one comment beats ten posts"; and all outreach reply-rate benchmarks. **The real numbers for your audience come from your own analytics after you start posting.**

## The 6 things that survived verification

1. **LinkedIn is where recruiters/alumni look first** (Jobvite: 95–97% of recruiters use it). The profile is not optional. **[VERIFIED 3-0]**
2. **The "golden hour" is real** — the algorithm tests a post in roughly the first **60–120 min**; good early engagement expands reach. Post when you can personally seed thoughtful early comments. **[VERIFIED core 3-0]**
3. **Consistency beats volume** — ~1–2 quality posts/week sustained for months beats burst-then-silence. Pick a fixed weekly content day. (1–2/week is a sustainable *floor*, not a ceiling.) **[VERIFIED 3-0]**
4. **Build-in-public content beats promotional content** — build-logs, teardowns, learnings, and data viz from your dashboard are the high-performing type. Showing work substitutes for seniority. **[VERIFIED 2-1]**
5. **Comment to warm up outreach** — leave *substantive* comments on a target's posts for **1–2 weeks before** any cold note. The direct bridge to Pillar-3 outreach. **[VERIFIED 3-0]**
6. **Portfolio links go in static profile fields** — Featured/About carry no feed penalty; post bodies do. Link the India MSME dashboard in **Featured + About**, keep post bodies link-free. **[VERIFIED 3-0]**

## What to do (practical)

- **Profile (highest verified ROI):** keyword-led headline for recruiter search; dashboard link in **Featured** and **About** (first 2–3 About lines are the hook); use the banner; complete the profile + free verification (the multiplier stats are refuted but the actions are cheap and sensible).
- **Content for a student with no title:** (1) dashboard build-logs incl. things you got wrong (the hardcoded-10x-vs-real-29.4x story is gold), (2) one chart + one paragraph of "what surprised me" (you already produce `chart_A`/`chart_B`), (3) PM-style product teardowns, (4) learning-in-public, (5) reflections tied to real action. **Specificity is the substitute for authority.**
- **Cadence/timing:** 1–2 posts/week on a fixed day; post when *you* have 60–90 min to reply to comments (your presence in the golden hour beats any "optimal clock time," all of which are refuted anyway).
- **Engagement:** comment substantively (but commenting does NOT replace posting — do both); reply to your own comments; **skip engagement pods** (unproven upside, real authenticity/detection downside).

## Anti-patterns

Broetry (fake one-line-per-sentence drama); engagement bait ("Comment 'PM' below"); inspirational-fiction stories; "Thrilled to announce" pure self-promo (keep ~80/20 value-to-promo); generic copy-paste praise; links jammed in post bodies; and **believing any post that quotes a precise benchmark** ("596% more", "post at 4 PM Wednesday") — that's the refuted folklore.

## Open questions (your analytics will answer these)
Best times/days for *your* audience; true size of the link penalty; which format actually performs; realistic warmed-up reply rate; engagement-pod risk/benefit. All had their published numbers refuted — measure your own.
