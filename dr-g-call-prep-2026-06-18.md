## 4. The crosswalk, in your own words (so you can say it cold)

Two datasets that were never built to connect. WTO trade data is coded in EBOPS (service categories, e.g. "other business services"). Udyam firm data is coded in NIC/ISIC (industries a firm sits in). To say "small firms in sector X are exposed to digital trade," you bridge EBOPS to ISIC with the WTO correspondence, e.g. "other business services" maps to ISIC L+M+N. The bridge tells you which codes correspond in principle. It does not tell you the actual firms in that industry are the ones exporting. No dataset records which individual Indian MSMEs trade digital services, so you're inferring exposure from "lots of firms here" plus "big trade in the mapped category," not measuring it. That's why it's direction, not magnitude, and why you flagged it. (Analogy if you need one: estimating which neighborhoods drink the most coffee by mapping coffee-shop product sales onto census data. You assume the locals are the buyers; you never watched a single purchase tied to an address.)
