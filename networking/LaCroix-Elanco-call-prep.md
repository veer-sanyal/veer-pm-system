## Read his message before you dial
He already told you the headline: **"no real strategy... mostly being in the right situation at the right time."** Do NOT fight that or fish for a hidden playbook — it reads as not listening. Instead mine it: what the right situation *looked like*, how he got into it, and what he did once he was there. "Luck" from the outside is usually positioning + readiness from the inside; your job is to surface the part he can actually pass on.

## Open (15–30 sec)
> "Thanks for making time, Steven — and for being straight that there wasn't some master plan. That's actually the part I want to understand: what the right situation looked like and how you ended up in it. Mind if I ask a few questions?"

## Core questions (pick 3–4; let each breathe)
Built off his own framing, not against it:
1. **"When you say right place, right time — what did that situation actually look like? What was happening on the team that opened the PM door?"**
2. **"What had you already built or shown that made you the person they moved into it, instead of someone else who was also around?"** (this is the positioning underneath the "luck")
3. **"Coming from data/stats, how much of the product-metrics and experimentation thinking did you have going in versus pick up on the job?"** (ties straight to your Pillar-2 gap — metrics/stats fluency is your #1 technical priority)
4. **"What in your data background turned out to transfer best to PM, and what did you have to unlearn?"**
5. **"If you were finishing undergrad again and wanted PM, what would you actually do this next year to be in the right situation on purpose?"**

## If it runs long / bonus
- "How did the Data Mine actually help — was it the projects, the corporate contacts, or something else?" (your direction.md flags the Data Mine as a network surface; get his read)
- "How do you spend your time as an APM day to day — how much is data work versus the rest?"
- One specific thing about his work you can react to genuinely if he raises it.

## Don't
- Don't push for a strategy he already said he doesn't have. Mine the situation instead.
- Don't ask for a referral or a job. Not this call.
- Don't ask anything a search answers (his title, what the Data Mine is).
- Don't dominate or over-explain your project. Curiosity > performance.

## Close (last ~1 min)
1. Genuine thanks for one specific thing he said.
2. **"Who else would you suggest I talk to?"** — manufactures warm intros, which reply far higher (70–90%) than cold sends. The single highest-leverage line in the call. (He came through the Data Mine — he likely knows other data-to-PM people.)
3. "Would it be alright if I kept you posted on how this goes?" — opens an event-based follow-up channel.
