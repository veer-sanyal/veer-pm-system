## The one goal of THIS call
Learn from someone who just cleared the bar, and become someone he's glad he talked to. Sequence is **learn → relationship → opportunity**. **No referral ask on this call** — it's a learn-stage touch, and an early referral ask lands badly. The referral, if any, comes a later cycle once he understands your fit and the APM windows open (Aug–Oct).

Talk ratio: **he talks ~80%.** Your job is good questions and listening, not pitching.

## About Daniel (so you don't ask Google-able things)

## Open (15–30 sec)
Warm, specific, set the frame so he knows it's low-stakes:
> "Thanks for making time, Daniel. I'm not here to pitch you anything — you just ran the exact process I'm pointing at, so I mostly want to hear how it actually went for you. Mind if I ask a few questions?"

## Core questions (pick 3–4; let each breathe)
Non-obvious, grounded in his lived experience — the kind a search can't answer:
1. **"What actually moved the needle in the APM loop for you — what do you think got you the offer versus other strong candidates?"**
2. **"What did you do sophomore/junior year that actually made you more credible for PM, and what felt like motion but didn't matter?"** (separates signal from busywork)
3. **"Where do early-career PM candidates usually underestimate the bar — what surprised you about what they were really testing?"**
4. **"How did your team evaluate early-career candidates?"** (this is also the honest groundwork that makes a referral askable *later* — you're learning their bar, not fishing)
5. **"If you were a sophomore again starting today, what would you actually do with this next year?"**

## If it runs long / bonus
- "How did you scope your first product area at Fidelity Labs once you started?"
- "Your data-to-product path — how much of the analytics/metrics depth did you build before vs. on the job?" (ties to your Pillar-2 gap)
- One specific thing about his work you can react to genuinely if he raises it.

## Your 2-sentence intro (only if he asks "what are you working on")
> "IBE sophomore, did a SWE internship at a fintech last summer. Right now I've shipped a live dashboard that scores India's MSME service sectors by exposure to a specific WTO digital-trade rule, and I'm working on the metrics/stats fluency PM loops test."
Keep it tight. Mention the dashboard once; don't narrate it.

## Don't
- Don't ask for a referral or a job. Not this call.
- Don't ask anything a search answers (his title, what Fidelity Labs is).
- Don't dominate or over-explain your project. Curiosity > performance.
- Don't wing the close.

## Close (last ~1 min)
1. Genuine thanks for one specific thing he said.
2. **"Who else would you suggest I talk to?"** — manufactures warm intros, which reply at a much higher rate (70–90%) than cold sends. Single highest-leverage line in the call.
3. "Would it be alright if I kept you posted on how this goes?" — opens an event-based follow-up channel.

## Within 24h after the call
Thank-you that references something specific he said + names the action you'll take on his advice (event-based, not a drip). Template:
> "Thanks again, Daniel — your point about [X] is already changing how I'm [doing Y]. I'll follow up when I've [taken the advice / shipped the thing]. Really appreciate the time."
Then: only message again when you have a real update.
