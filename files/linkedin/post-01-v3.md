## Post body

India's consulting exports grew 13x since 2005, to $92B. The rest of the category they're filed under barely moved.

I spent the last three weeks building a public dashboard around a question I couldn't find a sector-level answer to: which of India's small businesses (MSMEs) are most exposed to digital trade policy?

The usual story about India's digital services trade is about the big IT exporters. So I combined two datasets that don't normally get joined: WTO services trade data and India's official MSME registration data, crosswalked into a first-version exposure index by sector.

What topped the index surprised me. Not transport, not the categories I expected. "Other business services," which sounds like a residual bucket, ranked highest. And when I disaggregated it, one line was carrying the category: consulting and professional services, up 13x since 2005 to $92B in exports, while everything else in the bucket sat near $16B. The firms behind that line are consulting, accounting, legal, and architecture practices, which is exactly where India's small businesses concentrate.

So when digital trade policy shifts, a lot of the exposure lands on small professional-services firms, not just the giants the headlines cover.

The takeaway I keep coming back to: aggregate category labels hide the story. "Other business services" reads like a footnote. It contains the fastest-growing export line in the dataset.
