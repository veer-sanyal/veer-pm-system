- **[VERIFIED]** — survived 3-vote adversarial verification (2/3+ confirm). Directionally trustworthy.
- **[SOURCED–GENERAL]** — appears in a credible source and is consistent with the verified mechanics, but the *specific* claim wasn't independently verified. Use as practitioner guidance.
- **[INFERENCE]** — my reasoning connecting verified facts to your situation; not directly stated by a source.
- **[REFUTED]** — a commonly-repeated number that **failed** verification (0/3 or 1/2 confirm). Do **not** plan around these. Listed explicitly so you recognize and discount them when you see them online.

### The blunt headline up front

**Almost every precise LinkedIn benchmark you'll see online is marketing-blog folklore that does not survive scrutiny.** Of 25 high-value claims verified, **17 were killed.** The casualties include: every "best time/day to post" figure (Buffer, Hootsuite, and Sprout Social each published *conflicting* numbers, all refuted), carousel/video engagement multipliers ("596% more!", "5x video"), ideal character counts, the "60% external-link penalty," the dwell-time-over-likes weighting, the comment/share/reply weight rankings, the "21x more profile views," and LinkedIn outreach reply-rate benchmarks. Only **6 directional mechanics survived.** Plan around the mechanics; ignore the numbers. The real numbers for *your* audience come from your own LinkedIn analytics after you start posting. **[VERIFIED]**

## Executive summary (the 6 things that survived verification)

1. **LinkedIn is the first place recruiters, hiring managers, and alumni look** when they evaluate you — so the profile and your visibility there are not optional. Jobvite's Recruiter Nation data has 95–97% of recruiters using LinkedIn to find/evaluate candidates. This is *why* the whole strategy matters. **[VERIFIED, 3-0]**
2. **The "golden hour" is real.** The algorithm tests a new post on a slice of your network in roughly the first **60–120 minutes**; if early engagement is good, it expands reach. So post when you can personally seed thoughtful early comments. The *exact* window and the "2–5% of network" figure are unverified — treat it as "first hour or two," not a hard 60-minute gate. **[VERIFIED core, 3-0 / specifics 2-1]**
3. **Consistency beats volume.** ~1–2 quality posts/week sustained for months beats a burst of daily content followed by silence. A fixed weekly "content day" is standard practice. (Higher sustained frequency reaches more, so 1–2/week is a sustainable *floor* for a full-time student, not a ceiling.) **[VERIFIED, 3-0]**
4. **Personal, build-in-public content from a person beats promotional/brand content.** Project build-logs, teardowns, learnings, and data visualizations from your dashboard are exactly the high-performing, credibility-building type. Showing work substitutes for the seniority you don't have yet. **[VERIFIED, 2-1]**
5. **Posting + commenting warm up outreach.** Comment thoughtfully on a target alum/recruiter's posts for **1–2 weeks before** sending a cold note. Substantive comments only ("Great post!" doesn't count). This is the bridge from content to the Pillar-3 outreach funnel. **[VERIFIED, 3-0]**
6. **Park portfolio links in static profile fields, not in post bodies.** Featured and About are static profile elements, so the (real but unquantified) in-feed external-link reach penalty doesn't apply to them. Link the India MSME dashboard in **Featured** and **About**; keep post bodies link-free. **[VERIFIED, 3-0]**

## (1) What actually works in a post

### The verified mechanics
- **Hook matters because of the golden hour, not because of a character cutoff.** The first line has to earn the early comments that trigger reach expansion. (The specific "210 chars before *See more*" / "60–70% never click *See more*" numbers were **[REFUTED]** — but "lead with the strongest line" is sound regardless of where the fold actually falls.) **[INFERENCE]**
- **Format:** All format-multiplier claims were **[REFUTED]** (carousels "596% more", video "5x", document posts "6.6% vs 2%" — every one failed). So **there is no reliable format ranking.** **[INFERENCE]** Practical read: a clear text post with one strong visual (a chart from your dashboard) is a safe default; don't chase a format because a blog promised a multiplier. Test formats against your own analytics. **[SOURCED–GENERAL]**
- **Length:** "1,300–1,900 chars = 47% more engagement" was **[REFUTED]**. Write as long as the idea needs and no longer. **[INFERENCE]**
- **Hashtags:** No verified guidance surfaced. Practitioner consensus (unverified) is 3–5 relevant tags, not a wall. Treat as low-stakes. **[SOURCED–GENERAL]**

### Post structure that practitioners converge on (use as a starting template, not law) **[SOURCED–GENERAL]**
1. **Hook** — one specific, concrete line. Concrete beats clever.
2. **Context/tension** — the problem, the surprise, the thing you got wrong.
3. **The substance** — what you found/built/learned, with a real detail or number from *your* work.
4. **A takeaway** — the generalizable lesson.
5. **A light invitation** — a genuine question that invites a substantive reply (which feeds the golden hour). Avoid engagement-bait ("Comment 'YES' below").

### The "build-in-public" approach (your highest-leverage angle) **[VERIFIED that this content type works, 2-1]**
You have an unusually good build-in-public asset: a real dashboard with real decisions. Post the *process*, not just the launch:
- "I had a hardcoded 10x growth number in my dashboard. The real WTO figure was 29.4x. Here's why I rebuilt the chart and the rule I adopted: no hardcoded numbers." (You literally have this — `chart_B_ip_licensing.png`.)
- A weekly "what I learned building X this week" log.
- A teardown of a product you use, written like a PM would scope it.
- A data visualization with a one-paragraph "here's what surprised me."

## (2) The feed algorithm — what's real vs folklore

**Real (directional):** **[VERIFIED]**
- Early engagement in the first ~60–120 min ("golden hour") gates further reach.
- Engagement *quality* (comments/conversation) matters more than passive likes — though the precise weight ranking of comments > shares > saves > likes was **[REFUTED]** as a specific claim, the directional "comments/conversation help most" is consistent with the golden-hour mechanism. **[INFERENCE]**
- **Mid-2025 shift:** LinkedIn moved toward **relevance over pure recency** — older relevant posts can now resurface in feeds. So the golden hour is softening from a hard gate into *one factor* in continuous evaluation. A genuinely useful post has a longer tail than it used to. **[VERIFIED, in caveats]**

**Folklore — recognize and discount these:** **[REFUTED]**
- "External links cost ~60% reach" and "put the link in the first comment to dodge it" — *both* refuted. There is likely *some* link penalty (which is why Featured/About is the safe home for your portfolio link), but the **magnitude is unverified** and the first-comment workaround is not reliably established.
- "Dwell time outweighs likes (30s read > 50 quick likes)" — refuted as stated.
- "Reply to comments within 15 minutes for an algorithmic boost" — refuted. (Replying is still good for the conversation; just don't believe the 15-minute number.)

**What kills reach (defensible):** going silent for weeks then bursting; engagement-bait; pure self-promotion with no value; posting when no one you know is around to engage in the first hour. **[SOURCED–GENERAL / INFERENCE]**

## (3) Cadence, timing, consistency

- **Cadence: ~1–2 quality posts/week, sustained.** This is the verified floor. **[VERIFIED, 3-0]**
- **Fixed content day:** pick one (the source example used Thursday) and protect it like a class. Lowers activation energy and makes consistency a habit, not a decision. **[VERIFIED / INFERENCE]**
- **Best times/days: UNKNOWN — do not trust any published table.** Every Buffer/Hootsuite/Sprout "best time" figure was **[REFUTED]** *and they contradicted each other* (one said 4–6 AM Tue/Wed, another said 3–8 PM with Wed 4 PM, another said avoid Mon/Tue). **The empirical answer is in your own analytics.** Post at a few different times for 3–4 weeks, read *your* impression data, then standardize. **[VERIFIED that the benchmarks are unreliable]**
- **Repurposing:** a teardown → a carousel → a short "lesson" post is a reasonable way to get 3 posts from one piece of work. No verified data on this; treat as efficiency, not a reach hack. **[SOURCED–GENERAL]**
- **Timing that IS in your control:** post when *you* have 60–90 minutes free to reply to comments. The golden hour rewards your presence more than any clock time. **[INFERENCE from VERIFIED golden hour]**

## (4) Profile optimization (the part with the highest verified ROI)

The profile is the channel recruiters check first **[VERIFIED]**, and it's a static page with **no feed penalty** **[VERIFIED]** — so this is where your links and proof belong.

- **Headline:** keyword-led so you surface in recruiter Boolean/AI search (e.g. "Aspiring PM · IBE @ Purdue · building a data dashboard on India's services-trade shift"). Lead with the searchable role words; use the project as the secondary "prove impact" hook. **[VERIFIED, 3-0]**
- **Featured:** put the **India MSME dashboard link** here, plus your best post and any teardown. This is the penalty-free home for your portfolio URL — *do not* bury it in post bodies. **[VERIFIED, 3-0]**
- **About:** first 2–3 lines are the hook (they show before *See more* on the profile). A short "why product, what I'm building, what I'm looking for" story + the dashboard link. **[VERIFIED for portfolio-in-static-fields, 3-0]**
- **Banner:** free real estate — a clean shot of the dashboard or a one-line positioning statement reads more serious than the default blue. **[SOURCED–GENERAL]**
- **"All-Star" completeness / verification:** the famous "21x more profile views" stat was **[REFUTED]**, and so was a separate verified-member engagement stat. Completing your profile and getting the free identity verification are still cheap and sensible — just don't believe the specific multipliers. **[REFUTED specifics / INFERENCE the action is still worth it]**

## (5) Engagement strategy

- **Comment to grow — but the strong version was refuted.** "One thoughtful comment beats ten posts" was **[REFUTED]**. Commenting is valuable, but it does **not** replace posting — do both. **[REFUTED the trade-off claim]**
- **The warm-up tactic IS verified:** before any cold outreach, leave **substantive** comments on the target's posts for **1–2 weeks**. Prospects are meaningfully more likely to accept a request from someone who already engaged. Substance is the whole point — add a perspective, ask a real question, share a relevant data point. **[VERIFIED, 3-0]**
- **Respond to your own comments** to keep the conversation (and the golden hour) alive — just ignore the "within 15 minutes" myth. **[INFERENCE]**
- **Engagement pods:** **no verified evidence either way** on efficacy or detection risk. Given you're building a real reputation for a real recruiting goal, the asymmetry argues against them — the downside (looking inauthentic, possible detection) outweighs an unproven upside. Grow on substance. **[INFERENCE — open question]**

## (6) Content strategy for a student with no big title

Your disadvantage (no senior title) is neutralized by the verified finding that **build-in-public, show-your-work content outperforms brand/promotional content** **[VERIFIED, 2-1]**. Credibility comes from *demonstrated thinking*, not a job title. What to post, ranked by fit for you:

1. **Dashboard build-logs** — decisions, tradeoffs, things you got wrong and fixed (the hardcoded-number story is gold). Shows PM judgment in public.
2. **Data visualizations with a thesis** — you already produce these (`chart_A_consulting.png`, `chart_B_ip_licensing.png`). One chart + one paragraph of "what surprised me" is a complete post.
3. **Product teardowns** — pick a product, scope the problem, propose a change, like a PM would. Demonstrates the core skill recruiters screen for.
4. **Learning-in-public** — "I'm learning product metrics; here's the mental model that finally clicked." Sophomore-appropriate, honest, relatable.
5. **Reflections tied to real action** — what an alumni conversation taught you (no names without permission), what a recruiting milestone felt like.

**Credibility without seniority [INFERENCE]:** specificity is the substitute for authority. "Services exports from India grew 13x since 2005 while the rest of 'other business services' stayed flat — here's the chart and why it matters" reads as more credible than any "5 lessons on leadership" post from someone with no track record. Show the work; let the work be the authority.

## (8) Templates & anti-patterns

### A build-in-public template you can adapt (practitioner-derived, not a guaranteed format) **[SOURCED–GENERAL]**
> **Hook:** I had a wrong number sitting in my dashboard for two weeks.
>
> **Tension:** It said India's IP-licensing exports grew ~10x since 2005. I'd hardcoded it from a summary I half-remembered.
>
> **Substance:** When I pulled the actual WTO data and computed it, the real figure was 29.4x — nearly 3x what I'd written. [chart]
>
> **Takeaway:** I adopted one rule for the whole project: no hardcoded numbers, every figure traces to a source in the data layer. It's slower. It's also the difference between a dashboard and a slide.
>
> **Invitation:** For anyone who's built a data product — where do you draw the line between "good enough estimate" and "must be sourced"?

### Anti-patterns / cringe to avoid **[SOURCED–GENERAL]**
- **Broetry** — the "one line. per. sentence. for fake drama" format is widely mocked; don't fake-deep an ordinary point.
- **Engagement bait** — "Comment 'PM' and I'll send you my notes," "Agree? 👇". Reads as desperate and the algorithmic payoff is unproven.
- **Humble-brag / inspirational-fiction** — invented "a stranger taught me a lesson at the airport" stories. Recruiters can smell it.
- **Pure self-promotion** — "Thrilled to announce" with no value for the reader. Use the ~80/20 value-to-promotion ratio.
- **Generic copy-paste** — flattery and platitudes signal bulk activity. Specificity is the whole game.
- **Links jammed in the post body** — there's a real (if unquantified) feed penalty; put URLs in Featured/About/first-comment context instead. **[VERIFIED that static fields are the safe home]**
- **Believing the numbers** — any post that cites "596% more engagement" or "post at exactly 4 PM Wednesday" is repeating refuted folklore. **[REFUTED]**
