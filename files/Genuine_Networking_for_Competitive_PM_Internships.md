# Genuine Networking for Competitive PM Internships

For a college sophomore aiming at competitive PM internships or APM-style roles, the evidence supports a calmer view of networking than the internet usually gives you. Referrals do help, but they mostly help at the **top of the funnel** by getting your application seen or getting you into an interview. They are much less clearly decisive once structured interviews begin. At the same time, the best evidence on informational interviews, relationship-building, and social psychology points in the same direction: people respond best when outreach is specific, brief, respectful, and genuinely curious, and they are more receptive than most senders expect. ## Why cold outreach feels so much scarier than it actually is

The dread is not imaginary, but it is often miscalibrated. More than a decade of social-psychology work associated with Vanessa Bohns and colleagues shows that people systematically underestimate how willing others are to comply with direct requests. In the classic Flynn and Lake study, requesters underestimated compliance by **as much as 50%**. BohnsŌĆÖ review summarizes a broader body of work in which participants across many studies asked **more than 14,000 strangers** for different kinds of help and were consistently too pessimistic about their influence. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn15search2Ņłéturn15search19Ņłéturn15search4Ņłü

There is an important channel nuance, though: in a 2017 study, requesters **overestimated** compliance when asking over email, even though richer channels tend to work better.
